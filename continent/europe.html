Europe is a territory conventionally considered as a continent, bounded to the west by the Atlantic Ocean and the Greenland Sea, to the north by the Arctic Ocean. Its southern limit is marked by the Mediterranean Sea and the Strait of Gibraltar which separates it from Africa, while the Sea of Marmara (with the Dardanelles and the Peter the Great for the Ural Mountains, the Ural River, the north-west coast of the Caspian Sea and the Caucasus, is the traditional boundary, but remains, in the absence of a clear and precise separation, the subject of controversy over whether or not a certain number of countries belong to the European continent. Geographically, Europe can also be considered a part of the supercontinents of Eurasia and Afro-Eurasia.
In its most common sense, the European continent covers an area of about 10,180,000 km2 and has a population of about 743 million inhabitants: Europeans. It is the second smallest continent, covering just under 4% of the world's land surface.
