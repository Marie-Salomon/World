Thailand (Thai: ประเทศไทย) is a country in Southeast Asia. Before 1939, it was called the Kingdom of Siam. The latter is bordered to the southwest and northwest by Burma, to the south by Malaysia, to the southeast by Cambodia and to the northeast by Laos.

Culture

Thai culture, influenced by Buddhism and Hinduism, is very old. It shares many points in common with that of neighbouring countries

Religion

94.6% of Thais are Buddhists, 4.6% are Muslims, 0.7% are Christians, and the rest are of various religions. The Brahmanist pilgrimage site plays an important role in traditional Thai religion, influenced by India.

Custom

One of the Thai customs is the "Waï", a greeting gesture very similar to the Namaste of the Indians. Used for hello, Farewell(=adieu), it appears in different styles that all have the same meaning: normally a Thai makes a "bow(=révérence)" with his hands as a gesture of prayer.

Public displays of affection are common between friends but very rarely between men and women.Therefore, it is very rare to see couples kissing or holding hands. Touching someone's head or placing your foot at someone's head level is considered rude. Indeed, Thais consider the foot to be a dirty and coarse part of the body, while the head is a noble and elevated part. This influenced the way Thais sit; The feet are placed on the side of the body, a little hidden, avoiding showing them to others. Pointing to or touching things with the foot is considered a vulgar action.
