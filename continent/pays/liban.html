Lebanon's constitution is not secular but multi-confessional: the country has a political system based on a distribution of power proportional to the demographic weight of each faith, taken into account as a constituent community in the image of the old Ottoman system of "religious nations" or "millets".

Geography

Lebanon, a country in the Levant (Near East), is a narrow strip of land bordered by the Mediterranean Sea on 240 km of coastline. 250 km long and 25 to 60 km wide, it has a border with Syria for 376 km and with Israel, to the south, for 79 km. Its area is officially 10,452 km2, although this measure, which has significant political symbolism in Lebanon, is technically subject to debate
